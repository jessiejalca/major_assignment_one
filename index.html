What is Bauhaus?

description

Bauhaus was an artistic movement that started with Staatliches Bauhaus, a school founded by German architect Walter Gropius in 1919. His idea was to unify practical crafts and fine art disciplines and create a hub for creatives to work together. By 1923, it operated under the slogan "Art into Industry." Unfortunately, it was closed only 14 years later under Nazi pressure in 1933.

However, its influence persisted through its dissolved faculty and students throughout Europe and the United States. According to My Modern Met, "Bauhaus is often credited as the catalyst for modern architecture and furniture and as an important influence on mid-20th century painting and sculpture."

The Bauhaus style emphasized function and gave much attention to ease of use, but fused it together with art. As such, it featured a lot of abstract shapes and balanced geometric forms, bold colors and typography, clean lines and glass panels, simplicity and openness.
